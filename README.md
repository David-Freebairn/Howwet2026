Howwet2026 is a reenactment of a stand-alone Windows 1994 program designed to support farmers and consultants in estimating soil water status as part of their decisions making prior to planting crops. The focus was solely on water capture during fallows (non-crop period), an essential risk management tactic in a semi-arid environment
Howwet2026 applies a daily water-balance model (used in HowLeaky and SoilWaterApp)) which describes the dynamics of water in agricultural systems based on weather and soil type. Processes include infiltration of rainfall, runoff, evaporation and deep drainage to track plant available soil water daily.
Parameters describing soil properties are retrieved from a small database of representative soils for Australia.
